Summer Programming


This summer, I wanted to spend my time wisely and focus on programming. I find that I forget much of what I learn during the school year so I wanted to review and force myself to create something once a week. Every Saturday until September 1st 2018, I will be coding and uploading simple projects. These aren't really meant to be shown on a resume, but are fun little applications that keep me coding throughout the summer. If you have any suggestions, you can find my email on my github. If you would like to check out some of my larger projects, they are located here.

Week 1: 05/20-05/26

Dice roller where a selector moves across the board and highlights each die. When the timer runs out, the highlighted die is selected.

Week 2: 05/27-06/02

Convert your facebook friends list to a database. Comma separated values on the left, table on the right.

Week 3: 06/03-06/09

Paint program using p5.js. Change the color and size of your brush, then paint away!

Week 4: 06/10-06/16

Basic plotting application using p5.js. Type out the points on the right, and this graphs them on the left.

Week 5: 06/17-06/23

Creating a calculator in a new language is like a rite of passage. If you don't make a calculator in that language did you really learn it?

Week 6: 06/24-06/30

Test out some of the options for filter in CSS. Yes, that is my dog, Ben, and he is available for modeling.

Week 7: 07/01-07/07

Complete one of the most difficult tasks of the 21st century: unlocking a phone. Luckily the pin is right there.
